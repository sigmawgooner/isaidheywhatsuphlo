Afghanistan Proxy War (1979-1989)

Main Events

1979: Soviet troops enter Afghanistan

The Soviet Union invaded to support the communist government, starting a decade-long conflict.

1980s: US and Pakistan supply Mujahideen

The US and Pakistan provided weapons and training to rebel fighters opposing the Soviet-backed government.

1986: Guerrilla warfare intensifies

Fighting spread across Afghanistan with ambushes, bombing, and constant conflict.

1989: Soviet withdrawal

After heavy losses, Soviet troops pulled out, but civil war continued internally.

1992: Afghan government collapses

The communist government finally fell, leaving Afghanistan in chaos and paving the way for future conflicts.

Effects on Afghanistan

  • Destruction of cities and villages
  • Millions of refugees to Pakistan and Iran
  • Rise of extremist groups
