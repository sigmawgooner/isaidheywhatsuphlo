Angolan Civil War (1975-2002)

Main Events

1975: Angola gains independence

After Portuguese rule ended, rival factions MPLA and UNITA began fighting for control, drawing in foreign support.

1980s: Cuban and US involvement

Cuba sent troops to support MPLA, while the US and South Africa supported UNITA, escalating the conflict.

1991: Bicesse Accords

A temporary peace agreement was signed, but fighting resumed shortly afterward.

2002: Death of UNITA leader

Jonas Savimbi's death finally ended the long-running civil war.

Effects on Angola

  • Economic collapse
