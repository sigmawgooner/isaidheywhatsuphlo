Korean War (1950-1953)

Main Events

1950: North Korea invades South Korea

North Korean troops crossed the 38th parallel, starting the Korean War.

1950: UN counterattack

UN forces, led by the US, pushed North Korean forces back, nearing the Chinese border.

1950: Chinese intervention

China sent troops to support North Korea, pushing UN forces south.

1953: Armistice signed

The war ended in a ceasefire, dividing Korea near the 38th parallel (DMZ), but no formal peace treaty was signed.

Effects on Korea

  • High casualties
  • Destruction of infrastructure
  • Permanent division of North and South Korea

Effects on Surrounding Countries

  • Increased US presence in South Korea and Japan
  • Heightened tensions in East Asia
