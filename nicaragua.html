Nicaraguan Conflict (1979-1990s)

Main Events

1979: Sandinistas overthrow Somoza

The Sandinista revolution ended the Somoza dictatorship, creating a leftist government in Nicaragua.

1981: US begins supporting Contras

The US funded and trained Contras to fight the Sandinista government, escalating the conflict.

1980s: Civil conflict intensifies

Violent clashes, human rights abuses, and instability plagued Nicaragua throughout the decade.

1990: Sandinistas lose elections

US-backed opposition won the elections, ending Sandinista rule but leaving deep political divisions.

Effects on Nicaragua

  • Political polarization
  • Economic hardship
  • Infrastructure damage

Effects on Surrounding Countries

  • Increased US involvement in Central America
  • Refugee movement to neighboring countries
