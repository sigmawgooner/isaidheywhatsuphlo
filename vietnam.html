Vietnam Proxy War (1955-1975)

Main Events

1955: Vietnam divided into North and South

After the French left, Vietnam was split into a communist North and anti-communist South, setting the stage for a long conflict.

1964: Gulf of Tonkin incident

Alleged attacks on US ships gave the US Congress reason to escalate military involvement in Vietnam.

1968: Tet Offensive

A massive coordinated attack by North Vietnamese forces on South Vietnamese cities shocked the world and showed the war was far from over.

1973: Paris Peace Accords

Signed to end US combat involvement, the accords temporarily paused fighting, though conflicts continued between North and South Vietnam.

1975: Fall of Saigon

North Vietnamese troops captured Saigon, ending the war and reunifying the country under communist rule.

Effects on Vietnam

  • Massive casualties
  • Economic collapse
  • Environmental damage

Effects on Surrounding Countries

  • Destabilization of Laos and Cambodia
  • Rise of the Khmer Rouge
  • Increased US presence in Southeast Asia
